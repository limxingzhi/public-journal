The Current State

Right now as of 5th August 2022, I have two distinct apps for managing projects and resources. Things and Calendar. All actionable items will go into Things and all future events will go into Calendar. The problem now is, I do not have a system for storing long term knowledge. I am trying to address this problem with Bear, but let’s see how far it goes.


The Ideal scenario

This is the ideal setup I am trying to work towards.


Projects

A series of tasks linked to a goal with a deadline. They will have a specific endpoint. E.g. Writing a white paper or a family vacation


Area of Responsibility

A sphere of activities to be maintained overtime. They are not specific deadline-based tasks, but overarching aspects of my life. E.g. Work or Family or Health


Resource

A resource is a topic or theme of ongoing interest. They might be related or supporting my Projects / Area of Interest. E.g. Cooking or Travelling


Archive

Items from the above 3 that are no longer relevant, but still want to reference in the future


The Plan

This is how I am planning to get from the current state to the Ideal state.


Projects - Inbox + Calendar

Inbox is where I capture all my immediate and short term knowledge. Items that gets captured here includes

  • tasks with specific and approaching deadline
  • one-off tasks without long-term attachments

All items in the inbox are actionable, preferable with a timeframe. I will be using Things as my inbox, it provides enough power features and great UX for managing small tasks.


Areas of Responsibility

This will be spread across Things and Bear. On Things, they will sorted by their respective Headings in each “project” and on Bear, they will be sorted by their sub-tags.


Resources

Any short term resources like short reading will be stored on Things, any long term resources will be on Bear.
